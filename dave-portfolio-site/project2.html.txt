      <article>
        <h2>Overview</h2>
        <p>
          A growing web platform needed reliable insight into system health.
          Monitoring existed, but it was fragmented across tools, making it hard
          to answer basic questions like “Is it the app, the database, or the
          network?” and “Who is on point for this alert?”
        </p>

        <h2>Objectives</h2>
        <ul>
          <li>Centralize metrics, logs, and alerts into a cohesive observability stack.</li>
          <li>Define meaningful service-level indicators (SLIs) and objectives (SLOs).</li>
          <li>Enable engineers to diagnose incidents quickly and confidently.</li>
          <li>Support dashboarding for both technical teams and business stakeholders.</li>
        </ul>

        <h2>Solution</h2>
        <p>
          I introduced a standardized observability architecture built around
          Prometheus for metrics, Grafana for visualization, and a log aggregation
          backend (Loki/ELK) for application and infrastructure logs.
        </p>
        <p>
          Services exported metrics via language-appropriate client libraries,
          and common dashboards were created for latency, error rates, traffic,
          and saturation. Alerting rules were designed around SLOs instead of
          low-value “server is at 80% CPU” noise.
        </p>

        <h2>Key Responsibilities</h2>
        <ul>
          <li>Defined observability standards and instrumentation patterns.</li>
          <li>Deployed and tuned Prometheus, Grafana, and log aggregation.</li>
          <li>Worked with teams to add metrics to services and background jobs.</li>
          <li>Established alerting policies and on-call runbooks.</li>
          <li>Delivered training sessions on reading dashboards and debugging incidents.</li>
        </ul>

        <h2>Impact</h2>
        <ul>
          <li>Mean time to resolution (MTTR) significantly reduced for major incidents.</li>
          <li>Improved on-call experience with fewer noisy and duplicate alerts.</li>
          <li>Shared understanding of system health between dev, ops, and business teams.</li>
          <li>Foundation laid for error budgets and capacity planning.</li>
        </ul>
      </article>
